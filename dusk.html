Digital Dusk

The ultimate tactical manual for eliminating artificial light interference and reclaiming your biological evening.

The Culinary Zone Audit

  • Cover the oven, microwave, and coffee maker digital displays with monochromatic red vinyl film.
  • Mask the blue power LEDs on air purifiers and water filtration systems.
  • Disconnect or tape over the internal white LEDs in refrigerators; these are massive sources of vertical lux.
  • Verify that under-cabinet task lighting is either disabled or swapped for 670nm crimson strips.
  • Eliminate the use of smart screens or tablet recipe displays during the protocol window.

Living and Common Space Sanitation

  • Cover the standby lights on television panels, soundbars, and gaming consoles with light-blocking stickers.
  • Apply red tape over the high-frequency blue activity lights on internet routers and modems.
  • Replace overhead recessed bulbs with warm amber emitters (below 2000K) or clinical red sources.
  • Install heavy-gauge blackout curtains to eliminate nocturnal streetlamp and security light bleed.
  • Identify and mask the glowing status lights on smart home hubs and security panels.

Personal Device Hardening

  • Enable the Color Filter accessibility feature on smartphones to render the entire OS in monochromatic red.
  • Establish a hard-stop for all scrolling activities two hours before the protocol begins.
  • Utilize physical blue-light blocking eyewear (orange or red lenses) for any unavoidable screen exposure.
  • Disable raise-to-wake and notification-wake features on smartphones and tablets.
  • Switch all digital interfaces to true OLED Dark Mode to minimize total light emission.

Nocturnal Navigation Pathing

  • Install motion-activated 670nm red navigation lights along baseboards in hallways and bathrooms.
  • Ensure that nightlights are placed below knee level to minimize direct retinal exposure.
  • Audit the bathroom vanity; identify the lux output of mirror-integrated lighting and disable.
  • Remove all parasitic light sources (chargers, power strips) from the immediate sleeping area.
  • Set smart home automations to trigger only red-shifted light scenes after sunset.
