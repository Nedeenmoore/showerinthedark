"Dark Showering for Better Sleep: A Calming Trend Backed by Science"

The movement to reclaim our circadian biology starts with understanding the physiological damage caused by modern, hyper-stimulating environments. In a recent analysis, clinical psychologist Dr. Julie Kolzet outlines the exact science behind our protocol. She notes that bright, artificial light in the evening suppresses melatonin and keeps the nervous system artificially vigilant.

A Tactical Retreat

Lowering the lights is a necessary tactical retreat from the overstimulation of the commercial world. It forces a return to natural evening cues, allowing our minds to settle and our biological clocks to reset. This independent clinical insight is proof that the grassroots awakening to our circadian health is gaining momentum.
